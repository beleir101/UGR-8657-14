Axum

Axum, with its towering obelisks and archaeological wonders, became a journey through time, offering a glimpse into the heart of ancient Ethiopian civilization. The obelisks, remnants of a bygone era, stood tall against the backdrop of the St. Mary of Zion Church, adding a sense of mystery and majesty to the experience. The city's archaeological sites and the Queen of Sheba's Palace spoke volumes about Axum's significant role in shaping Ethiopia's rich heritage.

Fasieldes

Embarking on a journey to one of my countries wonder place Fasil, allowed me to step back in time and explore the historical marvels that this ancient city holds. The Royal Enclosure, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, unfolded tales of emperors and dynasties, leaving me in awe of the architectural prowess that graced the region centuries ago. From the majestic Fasil Ghebbi fortress to the enchanting Debre Berhan Selassie Church, every corner resonated with the vibrant history and culture of Ethiopia.

Vasa Museum

Venturing into Sweden was a captivating exploration of Scandinavian elegance and modern innovation. The streets of Stockholm echoed with a harmonious blend of historic charm and contemporary design. Visiting the Vasa Museum allowed me to witness a perfectly preserved 17th-century warship, while the Gamla Stan (Old Town) immersed me in the cobblestone alleys and colorful facades that define Sweden's capital. The seamless integration of tradition and progress was a testament to Sweden's cultural richness.

Norway Northern Lights

Northern Norway, with its ethereal landscapes and the allure of the Northern Lights, created an unforgettable travel experience. Standing beneath the shimmering auroras in Tromsø, I witnessed nature's own light show, a breathtaking dance of colors across the Arctic sky. The snow-covered landscapes and the crisp air added to the magic, making the Northern Lights expedition in Norway an adventure that transcended the ordinary and embraced the extraordinary.
